<p><ul><li>Temporary Cardiac Pacing</li><li>Single-Chamber Cardiac Pacing</li><li>Dual-Chamber Cardiac Pacing</li><li>Single-Chamber ICD</li><li>Dual-Chamber ICD</li><li>Three- Chamber CRT-P</li><li>Three- Chamber CRT-D</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cardiac pacing market encompasses various types that cater to different patient needs. Temporary cardiac pacing is used for short-term management in acute situations. Single-chamber pacing involves one lead, while dual-chamber pacing uses two to coordinate heart chambers. Implantable cardioverter defibrillators (ICDs) also come in single and dual-chamber forms for arrhythmia management. Additionally, three-chamber systems, CRT-P and CRT-D, offer advanced therapies for heart failure, improving synchronization and overall cardiac function. Each type addresses specific clinical scenarios and patient populations.</p></p>
