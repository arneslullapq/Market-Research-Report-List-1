<p><p>The automotive antifreeze liquid market primarily comprises three types: ethylene glycol, propylene glycol, and glycerin. Ethylene glycol is the most widely used antifreeze due to its effective heat transfer properties and low cost, though its toxicity poses safety concerns. Propylene glycol offers a safer alternative, being less toxic and environmentally friendly, making it suitable for food-grade applications. Glycerin, a biodegradable option, is gaining traction for its non-toxic properties, appealing to eco-conscious consumers. Each type serves distinct performance and safety requirements.</p></p>
