<p><p>The sour milk drink market encompasses various types, each with unique cultural significance and flavor profiles. Kefir, a fermented drink originating from the Caucasus, is rich in probiotics. Lassi, a traditional Indian beverage, can be sweet or savory and often includes fruits or spices. Doogh, popular in Persian cuisine, combines yogurt, water, and mint for a refreshing taste. Ayran, a Turkish drink, is a simple blend of yogurt, water, and salt. Other regional variations also contribute to this diverse market.</p></p>
